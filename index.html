Artifacts

Blazer, Skirts, and Scarf (1934)

This ensemble represents early 20th-century trends in women's sportswear and the growing acceptance of women's participation in athletics. The "Flaming Flashes" group highlights a bold identity centered around athleticism, challenging traditional gender roles.

Tennis Dress, Worn by Billie Jean King During the "Battle of the Sexes"

Billie Jean King's tennis dress is an artifact from a pivotal moment in the fight for gender equality in sports. The "Battle of the Sexes" match against Bobby Riggs in 1973 captivated the nation and became a symbolic victory for women athletes. Native American Prom Dress

This dress, while lacking specific details in the provided excerpt, represents the expression of cultural identity through fashion. This dress likely reflects individual and communal traditions while simultaneously engaging with mainstream American culture. Minnijean Brown Trickey's 1959 High School Graduation Dress

This dress embodies the resilience and determination of Minnijean Brown Trickey, one of the Little Rock Nine who bravely integrated Central High School in 1957. Despite enduring harassment and ultimately being expelled, Trickey graduated on schedule from New Lincoln School in New York City. The dress, designed by Trickey herself, symbolizes her personal triumph over adversity and the ongoing struggle for racial equality in education. The Smithsonian description notes that the dress "fit perfectly and helped boost her confidence; her graduation photograph was published by several New York papers."

Timeline of Events

  • 1934: A member of the Flaming Flashes wears a blazer, skirt, and scarf.
  • 1950: Althea Gibson becomes the first Black woman to play in the U.S. Tennis Championships.
  • 1957: Althea Gibson wins Wimbledon, marking a historic milestone for African-American athletes.
  • 1959: Minnijean Brown graduates from New Lincoln School in New York City, designing her own graduation dress.
  • 1964: Althea Gibson breaks racial barriers in professional golf by joining the LPGA.
  • Undated: Billie Jean King competes in the "Battle of the Sexes," wearing her iconic tennis outfit.

Cast of Characters

  • Althea Gibson: The first Black woman to win Wimbledon and break barriers in tennis and golf.
  • Billie Jean King: Renowned tennis player and advocate for gender equality, famously winning the "Battle of the Sexes."
  • Minnijean Brown Trickey: A member of the Little Rock Nine who stood against racial segregation in schools.
  • Mamie Phipps Clark: Social psychologist and civil rights activist, mentor to Minnijean Brown.
  • The Flaming Flashes: A 1934 women's athletic group whose uniform symbolized their bold identity.
